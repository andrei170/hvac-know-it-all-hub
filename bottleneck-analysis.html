Bottleneck Analysis - Gary McCreadie / HVAC Know It All

Built: 2026-05-17 Frame: Cold first-touch. No prior outreach. The structural map - the five things I would build first if I were stepping into the HVAC Know It All funnel today. Scope: The gap between a 101K-Instagram + multi-platform HVAC-educator audience plus an app already pulling 1,800+ installs in 1.5 weeks - and the productised paid tier the audience is signalling readiness for but cannot currently buy.

The one-sentence diagnosis

HVAC Know It All has built every audience layer (101K Instagram, weekly podcast, multi-year archive, mobile app pulling 1,800+ early installs, free Facebook community) and zero visible paid layers. The audience is already crossing from "I watch" to "I install a Gary-owned tool." The paid mid-tier for the HVAC shop owner and the early-career business-track technician is the missing revenue line, and the four-piece install patches the funnel underneath the audience without touching the content cadence.

The five things I would build first (ranked by leverage)

One. The paid mid-tier for the HVAC shop owner and the business-track technician is the missing revenue line.

The visible funnel is content + community + app, all free. The HVAC Know It All app launched and pulled 1,800+ users in 1.5 weeks. That conversion rate from social-audience-watching to free-tool-installing is the leading indicator the audience is willing to cross deeper into a Gary-owned environment. Right now, the deepest paid surface is sponsor-driven brand-partner revenue. The audience-to-revenue line goes through advertisers, not through a productised paid tier the audience can buy directly.

The fix is the HVAC Know It All Business Track - a productised paid tier built for the HVAC shop owner, the senior tech ready to launch a shop, and the established small-shop owner who needs the sales / pricing / ops / marketing scaffolding. Two parallel cohort tracks, both fronted by Gary in his existing operator-grounded voice:

  • Business Track A (Launch Smarter). 8 weeks. For the senior technician ready to start the shop or the first-year shop owner. Modules: licensing + business setup audit (province / state-aware), pricing structure that survives a slow week, the first 5 service-call leads and how to get them, basic CRM + scheduling, the hire-the-first-tech checklist, the first 90 days of cash management. Caps at 25-30 per cycle.

  • Business Track B (Scale Smarter). 6 weeks. For the established 1-to-5-tech shop owner ready to break the owner-on-tools ceiling. Modules: route optimisation, hiring + retention in the 2026 labour market, productised service agreements + recurring revenue, basic Meta + Google ads for local service, the owner-off-tools transition plan. Caps at 25-30 per cycle.

The two tracks are sequential for the same operator over a 12-month arc. The cadence is quarterly. AG1 builds the funnel infrastructure for the tier Gary is structurally ready to launch - we do not write the curriculum, we wire the path the audience uses to find it, decide on it, apply to it, and buy it.

Year-1 floor (conservative, two cohort tracks live by Q3):

  • Track A: 3 cycles x 25 seats x mid-four-figure pricing = floor in the $150K-$225K band
  • Track B: 3 cycles x 25 seats x mid-four-figure pricing = floor in the $150K-$225K band
  • Combined direct: $300K-$450K Year-1 incremental

These are floor numbers based on the visible audience size, the app-install velocity, and comparable productised education in the trades-business segment. The Business Track is positioned as operator development for the shop owner, not as ticketed trade training (which stays with the provincial colleges and the manufacturer-certified programs). Results vary by market, capital, and operator execution.

AG1 service-mapped wedge: AG1 builds the full funnel infrastructure for the paid tier - the VSL, the landing page, the application form, the email sequences, the segmented welcome flow, the payment integration, the post-purchase onboarding. Gary writes the curriculum and runs the cohort. We do not invent the cohort - we wire the surface the audience uses to find it.

Two. No VSL on hvacknowitall.com, no VSL on the app, no VSL on any social bio link - the audience converts (or does not) on bullet points alone.

The current public surface routes the audience from YouTube / Instagram / TikTok / LinkedIn to either hvacknowitall.com, the Facebook group, the app store, or the podcast index. None of these surfaces carry a 5-7 minute pre-sell video that frames the Business Track tier, explains who it is for, walks the work, and points to the application. The buy decision (when the paid tier launches) would currently happen on a static text-and-image landing page with no operator-grounded video walk-through.

The fix is a single 5-7 minute homepage VSL filmed in Gary's existing operator-educator register, embedded on hvacknowitall.com hero, on the Business Track landing page hero, and embedded in the mobile app's "About the Business Track" view. The script frames the Track A / Track B split, walks who it is for, names the work, and routes to the application.

Year-1 floor on the VSL alone (pre-sell lift on a launched paid tier):

  • Conservative pre-sell lift of 15-30% on cold-traffic-to-application conversion across the paid-tier landing: $50K-$120K Year-1 incremental on the cohort line
  • Compounds against every YouTube end-screen card, every Instagram bio click, every podcast outro, every Facebook group post, every app discovery view

AG1 service-mapped wedge: AG1 builds the 5-7 minute VSL - script in Gary's voice, direct the record, edit the cut, embed on all three surfaces, set captions for the 80% of YouTube and Instagram traffic that watches with sound off. The voice on screen is Gary's. We are not the talent; we are the infrastructure.

Three. The free HVAC Know It All app is a paid-tier discovery surface, currently set up only as a content surface.

The app launched and pulled 1,800+ users in 1.5 weeks. That is the highest-intent slice of the audience - they crossed from social-watching to tool-installing. Right now the app is content + community access. The structural opportunity is to wire the app as the primary discovery surface for the Business Track paid tier without changing the free-tier value the existing 1,800 users came for.

The fix is the in-app Business Track tile - a discovery view, not a paywall. The user opens the app, sees the free tier (content, community, tools), and also sees a clearly-labelled "Business Track - Apply" tile that routes to the scorecard, the VSL, and the application. The free tier stays free. The paid tier is surfaced inside the app, not hidden behind it.

Year-1 floor on the in-app discovery surface:

  • App-routed applications at 5-10% of the 1,800 base over Year-1, plus the compounding install velocity: $20K-$60K Year-1 incremental on the cohort line
  • The in-app surface compounds: every new app install increases the discovery base for the paid tier at no incremental acquisition cost

AG1 service-mapped wedge: AG1 wires the in-app discovery flow - we work with Gary's app developer on a single tile + deeplink + tracking pixel that routes to the Business Track scorecard. We do not rebuild the app. We add one surface.

Four. No email list, no scorecard, no lead magnet - the 101K Instagram + 1,800 app users + YouTube + Facebook audience exits the funnel untracked.

There is no visible Gary-owned email list. The Linktree-style bio routing, the app, the podcast, the Facebook group, and the social channels all capture audience attention without capturing email into a tracked, segmented, Gary-owned list. There is no quiz, no scorecard, no PDF download, no calculator on any public surface.

Every Instagram follower who watches a podcast clip and lands on the bio link exits the funnel without entering a list that can be nurtured back into the Business Track when the cohort opens.

The fix is the "Where Is Your HVAC Shop In The Growth Ladder?" scorecard - a 90-second 12-question readiness quiz returning a four-band result: Too Early (still in trade school or under 2 years on tools, route to free content), Ready For Track A (senior tech ready to launch a shop or first-year shop owner), Ready For Track B (1-to-5-tech shop owner ready to scale), Too Far Along (established 10+ tech shop owner past the cohort fit). Captures email. Tags by capital band, by tech-headcount band, by province / state, by current revenue band, by product-track preference.

Year-1 floor on the email asset alone:

  • Audience-to-email conversion at a conservative 3-6% over a 90-day window across YouTube end-screens, Instagram bio routing, app routing, and Facebook group routing: 5K-15K incremental email subscribers Year-1
  • Cohort fills sourced from the list (compounds each cycle): $25K-$70K Year-1 incremental
  • Sponsor / brand-partner upside from the segmented list (sponsor-targeted segments command higher CPM): $10K-$30K Year-1 incremental
  • Combined incremental email-asset floor: $35K-$100K Year-1

The list is the asset that turns every future cohort cycle from "depends on the YouTube algorithm and the Instagram reach" to "open the segmented list, run the scorecard-to-application nurture, fill the cohort."

AG1 service-mapped wedge: AG1 builds the scorecard quiz (question logic, scoring algorithm, four-band routing, email tagging), wires it into a Gary-owned email platform (we recommend ConvertKit or GHL based on existing tech stack), drafts the 5-email cold-first-touch nurture sequence, and sets the routing rules so each scored band gets the right next-step email.

Five. No segmented welcome flow underneath the free app users, the free community members, and (when launched) the paid-tier buyers.

The current public surface does not segment users by source or by intent. The app users get the same treatment as the Facebook group members and as the podcast subscribers. When the Business Track launches, the buyers will route into the same downstream as the free-app users unless a segmented welcome flow is wired underneath.

The fix is a segmented welcome flow that branches on three triggers: free-app-install (welcome to the free tier, here is the content map, here is the scorecard if you are curious about the Business Track), scorecard-completion (forked by score band into four nurture paths), and Business-Track-purchase (welcome to your track, here is the onboarding sequence, here is the calendar for the kickoff cohort call).

Year-1 floor on the segmented welcome flow:

  • Free-app-to-scorecard-to-cohort conversion lift from segmented nurture vs. flat one-size-fits-all welcome: $15K-$40K Year-1 incremental
  • Post-purchase onboarding lift (reduced refund / chargeback rate, lifted alumni satisfaction): $5K-$15K Year-1 incremental
  • Combined incremental welcome-flow floor: $20K-$55K Year-1

AG1 service-mapped wedge: AG1 wires the segmented welcome flow inside the email platform - branching logic, copy for each branch, integration with the app webhook (for free-app installs) and the purchase webhook (for Business Track buyers). Gary's voice on every email; we draft the copy and Gary signs off.

What does not need to change

  • The technician-to-technician voice on YouTube, podcast, Instagram, and the Facebook group
  • The weekly podcast cadence
  • The HVAC Hub Facebook group as the free community lane
  • The mobile app's free tier (the paid-tier discovery tile is additive, not a paywall on existing free features)
  • The sponsor / brand-partner revenue line (it stays as a parallel revenue lane, not replaced)
  • The Combined Air Mechanical day-job framing (it is part of the credibility wall - "still on the tools," not "left the trade to teach")
  • Gary's positive-environment brand line ("Learn, Teach, Entertain") - that is the cohort positioning, in his own voice, already public

What I would install in less than 14 days from kickoff

  1. The HVAC Know It All Business Track landing page (Track A + Track B side by side) deployed with the Typeform application form wired to a webhook
  2. "Where Is Your HVAC Shop In The Growth Ladder?" scorecard quiz live on a subdomain, integrated to the email list
  3. The 5-7 minute homepage VSL filmed (Gary's voice), edited, captioned, and embedded on hvacknowitall.com hero + landing hero
  4. Linktree / bio link routing updated: Business Track landing as the primary, scorecard as the secondary capture surface, app and free community as the tertiary
  5. 5-email cold-first-touch nurture sequence built and loaded into the email platform, triggered on scorecard completion
  6. Segmented welcome flow wired underneath three triggers (free-app install, scorecard completion, Business Track purchase)
  7. Pixel + GA4 firing on both surfaces with UTM tags off every YouTube end-screen card, every Instagram bio link, every Facebook post, every podcast outro

The slower-lever items - paid Meta / Google ads to the cohort, alumni community launch, Patreon-style monthly recurring tier, paid retargeting - are Day-60 plus, not Day-14. They are not on the install clock.

Compliance footer (trades / regulated-work language)

HVAC and refrigeration training touches regulated trades work (gas tickets, refrigerant handling, electrical) under provincial and state regulators (TSSA in Ontario, EPA Section 608 in the US, provincial trade colleges across Canada). The Business Track in this package is positioned as business-owner training (sales, pricing, ops, hiring, marketing) - not as a substitute for ticketed trade training. Trade-ticket curriculum stays with the provincial colleges and the manufacturer-certified programs. The cohort sells operator development for the shop-owner-track technician, not certification.

Nothing in this package promises a specific revenue number for the participant. Nothing in this package promises a specific hire rate, close rate, or business-growth outcome. The plan does not claim certification authority. Results vary by market, capital, operator execution, and regulatory environment.
