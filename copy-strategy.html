Foundation

Copy Strategy - Gary McCreadie / HVAC Know It All

Built: 2026-05-17 Frame: Voice rules, niche-bans, register separation, and the hero claims locked across the package. The voice on every Gary-facing surface is Gary's. The voice on every AG1-built surface mirrors that register without imitating it.

The one-sentence voice rule

Technician-to-technician, shop-owner-to-shop-owner. Plain English. No motivational-guru register. No flashy lifestyle B-roll. No "transform your business" framing. The voice on the page sounds like the voice on the podcast - operator-grounded, time-on-tools-respected, and built around what a working tech actually wants from a paid tier.

Voice register: what to hit, what to skip

Hit

  • Operator-grounded register: "20 years on the tools," "what survives a slow week," "the first hire," "the owner-off-tools transition," "the pricing structure that does not break"
  • Trades-craft respect: "the work" instead of "the hustle," "the tools" instead of "the lifestyle," "the shop" instead of "the agency"
  • Practical specificity: name the work, name the modules, name the cap, name the cadence
  • Canadian / regional honesty: TSSA where TSSA is relevant, EPA Section 608 where it is relevant, provincial colleges named where they are the right next-step routing
  • Sponsor / brand-partner respect: the existing sponsor relationships stay editorially independent from the Business Track marketing
  • Positive-environment brand line: every surface earns the "Learn, Teach, Entertain - positive environment" framing Gary's brand has built

Skip (the trade-business education vocabulary that breaks the brand)

  • "Six-figure HVAC business" (income-claim framing - banned in trades-business education marketing)
  • "Make your first $1M in HVAC" (income-claim framing - banned)
  • "Trade secrets" / "insider secrets" / "the secret to" (anti-trades-craft register - the audience reads it as a tell)
  • "Quick wins" / "fast cash" / "easy money" (anti-trades-craft register, regulator-flag in trades-business)
  • "Side hustle" framing (the audience are not side-hustlers - they are licensed technicians and shop owners)
  • "Mindset coaching" / "millionaire mindset" / "abundance" (motivational-guru register that does not fit Gary's voice)
  • "Game-changer," "revolutionary," "unlock," "transform," "leverage," "level up" (general AI-tell vocabulary banned across all AG1 deliverables)
  • "Disrupt the trades" / "disrupt HVAC" (anti-trades-craft register - the audience is the trades, not the disruptor)

Banned phrases (package-wide)

  • Em-dashes (U+2014) - zero. Use hyphens, colons, or new sentences.
  • "Game-changer," "revolutionary," "transformational," "unlock your potential," "transform your life," "in today's fast-paced world," "delve into," "paradigm shift," "synergy," "leverage synergies," "harness the power," "seamlessly," "effortlessly," "elevate your," "cutting-edge," "state-of-the-art," "next-level"
  • "Trade secrets," "insider secrets," "the secret to"
  • "Six-figure HVAC business," "make your first $1M in HVAC," any specific income claim for the participant
  • "Side hustle," "quick wins," "easy money," "fast cash"
  • "Mindset coaching," "millionaire mindset," "abundance," "manifest"
  • Minute-quantified CTAs in the cold IG DM, the cold LinkedIn DM, or the cold email opener (no "20-minute call," "15-min call," "30-minute consult")
  • Explicit AG1 pricing on the public landing-page.html (no $X,000, no $X/mo)

Compliance + regulated-niche register

  • The Business Track is operator development for the shop-owner-track technician. The cohort sells operator development, not certification.
  • Trade-ticket curriculum stays with the provincial colleges + manufacturer-certified programs (TSSA, EPA Section 608, NATE, provincial trade colleges across Canada). The cohort marketing acknowledges this and does not market against it.
  • Income / outcome language: replace specific-revenue claims with deliverable language. Examples:
  • Avoid: "Make your first $100K in HVAC"
  • Use: "Walk out with a written pricing structure that survives a slow week, a first-90-day cash plan, and a hire-the-first-tech checklist"
  • Footer on every regulated-niche surface (landing, hub, scorecard, every email): "Operator-development cohort. Not a substitute for ticketed trade training (TSSA / EPA Section 608 / provincial trade colleges / manufacturer-certified programs). Results vary by market, capital, and operator execution."

Voice notes

  • The cold-touch register (IG DM, LinkedIn DM, email opener) is operator-grounded and direct. No motivational framing. No "I have been following you for years" opener. No flattery. The wedge is the observation: built every audience layer, no visible paid tier, here is the structural fix.
  • The hub / landing / scorecard register is in Gary's voice, drafted by AG1, signed off by Gary on day one.
  • The VSL register is Gary on camera, AG1 script as first draft. The script does not put words in Gary's mouth that do not match his existing podcast register. Gary edits freely on the record day.
  • The email sequence register is Gary's signature block at the end ("- Gary, HVAC Know It All"), AG1 drafts the body in Gary's voice. Gary signs off line-by-line.
  • No echo of any other HVAC creator's signature phrasing. The cold copy does not borrow framing from AC Service Tech, HVAC School, or TruTechTools.
  • No promise of trading-style returns, no promise of a specific monthly revenue figure for the participant, no promise of a guaranteed hire. Results vary.
  • Compliance footer present on every public-facing AG1-built surface (hub, landing, scorecard, every email in the sequence).
