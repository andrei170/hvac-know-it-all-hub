Canadian HVAC education landscape (context)

Gary operates from Mississauga, Ontario. The Canadian HVAC trade is regulated province-by-province (TSSA in Ontario for gas, provincial colleges of trades for refrigeration / gas / oil tickets). Online HVAC training brands that compete for attention with HVAC Know It All include AC Service Tech LLC (Craig Migliaccio, US, ~250K YouTube), TruTechTools (sponsor/tool retailer + education partner), HVAC School (Bryan Orr, US, ~100K YouTube + podcast), and a small set of Canadian-specific trainers. Gary's structural moat is the Canadian-tech-operator perspective + the long-running positive-community brand + the multi-platform footprint (most peers are single-channel).

What no major HVAC-education creator has fully built yet is a productised paid-mid-tier specifically for the HVAC business owner / shop owner segment (vs. the technician-learning segment). The technician-learning segment is well-served by manufacturer training + AC Service Tech YouTube + HVAC School podcast + provincial college coursework. The shop-owner / installer-business-owner segment is structurally underserved on the education side.

App launch context (load-bearing detail)

The HVAC Know It All mobile app launched recently with 1,800+ users in the first 1.5 weeks (per Gary's public note). That number matters because:

Compliance + tone footer

HVAC and refrigeration training touches regulated trades work (gas, refrigerant handling, electrical) under provincial / state regulators (TSSA in Ontario, EPA Section 608 in the US, provincial trade colleges across Canada). The paid tier in this package is positioned as business-owner training (sales, pricing, ops, hiring, marketing) - not as a substitute for ticketed trade training. Trade-ticket curriculum stays with the provincial colleges and the manufacturer-certified programs. The cohort sells operator development for the shop-owner-track technician, not certification.
