Phase 3

Content Strategy - Gary McCreadie / HVAC Know It All

Built: 2026-05-17 Frame: No new content load on Gary. The strategy is to route existing surfaces (YouTube, Instagram, podcast, Facebook group, app, LinkedIn) to the new paid-tier funnel without changing the cadence, the format, or the voice.

The one-sentence content principle

The content cadence stays exactly as it is. The only change is where the bio link, the YouTube end-screen card, the podcast outro, the Instagram pinned story, and the in-app discovery tile point to.

The principle: no surface change requires Gary to film new content, write a new blog post, or change the podcast format. The change is in the routing layer underneath the existing content.

YouTube (the highest-leverage routing change)

YouTube is the surface where the audience self-selects deepest into the brand. Gary's weekly podcast video uploads carry the highest dwell-time and the highest comment-engagement per follower. The routing change:

  1. Pinned comment on every episode. Format: "If you are a senior tech or shop owner ready to launch / scale smarter, the Business Track is open. Take the 90-second scorecard - [scorecard URL]." Gary's existing comment-pinning workflow takes 10 seconds per upload.
  2. End-screen card on every episode. Replace the current "subscribe + app download" with "Business Track scorecard" as the primary card, app download as the secondary.
  3. Episode description first-line update. First line of every description becomes the Business Track landing URL with one-sentence context. The rest of the description stays as-is.
  4. One dedicated solo episode per quarter. Format: "What I Would Build If I Were Starting An HVAC Shop In [Year]" - Gary solo, 30-40 min, walking the Business Track curriculum at a 10,000-ft view. This is the cohort sales asset for evergreen YouTube viewers. One per quarter, not a cadence change.

The four-step routing change drives the YouTube audience into the scorecard without disrupting the guest-podcast cadence that built the channel.

Instagram + TikTok (the highest-volume routing change)

Instagram at 101K is the highest-volume surface. The routing change here is the bio link layer + the pinned Stories highlight, not the feed cadence. The four pieces:

  1. Linktree update. Business Track landing pinned at top with primary visual treatment. Scorecard second. App + Facebook group third and fourth.
  2. Pinned Stories highlight. Single "Business Track" highlight - 6-8 frames walking who it is for, the work, the application. Linked to the landing. Replaces a current highlight if one is at capacity.
  3. One sales-anchor Reel per month. Format: Gary speaking to camera, 60-90 sec, "If you are a senior tech ready to start your own shop, here is what I would tell you." Pinned to the grid. Replaces no existing content - just one new pinned anchor per month.
  4. Story repost from the podcast every Monday. Format: A 15-sec clip from the week's podcast, with the scorecard link as the swipe-up / sticker. Drives the highest-intent audience to the scorecard inside the podcast hype cycle.

TikTok mirrors the Linktree update + the one-Reel-per-month sales anchor (cross-posted from Instagram).

Podcast (the highest-trust routing change)

The HVAC Know It All Podcast is the surface with the deepest trust-per-listener. Listeners self-select for 30-60-minute audio commitment, which is the highest-intent audience signal in the brand. The routing change:

  1. Pre-roll mention (15 sec) on every episode. Format: "Quick note before we get into the episode - we launched the Business Track for senior techs and shop owners. If you are ready, the scorecard is in the show notes." 15 sec. Pre-recorded once per quarter, swapped in at edit.
  2. Mid-roll mention (one slot, 30 sec) on every episode. Format: Gary's voice, 30 sec, walking who the Business Track is for. Recorded once per quarter, swapped in at edit. Does not replace existing sponsor mid-rolls - sits in the slot the brand owns.
  3. Show notes top link. Business Track landing as the first link in every episode's show notes across Apple, Spotify, and the website index. Existing show notes content stays as-is below.
  4. Quarterly solo episode. Same as the YouTube solo episode above - audio-only repurpose of the same 30-40-min solo recording. One per quarter.

The four-step podcast routing change does not require any change to the guest-booking workflow or the recording cadence.

Facebook HVAC Hub group (the highest-conversion-density routing change)

The HVAC Hub is the brand's free community. Conversion density per member is structurally higher in a Facebook group than on a passive social platform - members are self-identified as technicians and shop owners and engage in operator-grounded conversations. The routing change:

  1. Pinned post update. Single pinned post: "We built a free 90-sec scorecard - find out which Track (Launch Smarter / Scale Smarter) fits your shop. Take it here - [scorecard URL]." Stays pinned.
  2. Weekly Tuesday post (Gary or VA). Format: A question that sparks operator discussion ("What is the pricing structure that survived your slowest week?"). At the end of the comment thread, Gary or the VA drops the scorecard link. Drives intent without breaking the community vibe.
  3. Monthly "Office Hours" thread. Format: Open Q&A thread, Gary answers 10-15 operator questions across the day. At the end, "Scorecard is here if you want the structured next step - [URL]."

No paid promotion inside the group. The group stays free.

In-app (the highest-intent surface)

The mobile app is the surface where the user has already crossed from "I follow Gary" to "I installed Gary's tool." The routing change:

  1. Business Track discovery tile on the app home view. Single tile. Routes to the in-app VSL preview + scorecard CTA.
  2. In-app push notification (one per cohort cycle). Format: "Business Track Cohort Q3 opens [date] - take the scorecard to apply." One push per quarter, not weekly.
  3. In-app post-onboarding screen update. When a new user finishes the first-open flow, the second screen presents the Business Track tile with a single sentence: "If you run a shop or are building one, take the scorecard."

App routing requires coordination with Gary's app developer - in scope is a single tile + a single deeplink + a single push notification template, not an app rebuild.

Email list (the asset built underneath all the surfaces above)

Email is the asset that turns every YouTube end-screen card, every Instagram bio click, every podcast outro mention, every Facebook group post, and every in-app tile into a tracked, segmented, Gary-owned contact. The list is built by the scorecard - the scorecard is the lead-magnet that captures the email at the moment of intent.

Once the list is at 5K-15K segmented contacts (12-month horizon, per the bottleneck analysis), the email layer becomes the primary cohort-fill mechanism for every future cycle. The content cadence above is what populates the scorecard with traffic. The list is the residue.

What the new routing does NOT touch

  • The guest-booking cadence and the weekly podcast format
  • The Instagram feed posting rhythm
  • The YouTube upload cadence
  • The Facebook HVAC Hub group culture and moderation rhythm
  • The app's free-tier value proposition
  • The Combined Air Mechanical day-job framing
  • The sponsor / brand-partner editorial line on the website + the podcast
  • The positive-environment brand line ("Learn, Teach, Entertain")

The strategy is additive on the bio link layer, the end-screen card layer, the pinned-comment layer, the pinned-post layer, the show-notes layer, and the in-app tile layer. Nothing in the strategy asks Gary to change what he posts, when he posts, or how he speaks.

Measurement

  • Primary metric: Scorecard completions per week (target: 50-150 in month one, scaling to 300-700 in month six as audience routing matures).
  • Secondary metric: Scorecard-to-application conversion rate (industry benchmark in trades-business education: 8-15%).
  • Tertiary metric: Application-to-paid conversion rate per cohort cycle (benchmark: 40-65% on a properly-scored quiz).
  • Tracking surface: Plausible or Fathom on the landing + scorecard subdomain, UTM tags on every routing surface (YouTube card / Instagram bio / podcast notes / Facebook post / in-app tile), webhook from the scorecard into the email platform with full segmentation tags.

The measurement layer is the second piece AG1 installs (after the landing + VSL). Without it, the content routing changes above are unmeasured.

Compliance footer

The content routing strategy does not promise a specific revenue number for the participant. The Business Track is positioned as operator development for the shop-owner-track technician. The content surfaces do not market the cohort as ticketed trade training. Results vary by market, capital, and operator execution.
